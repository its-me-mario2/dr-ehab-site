Dr. Ehab El Zahed is a specialized orthopedic surgeon based in Giza, Egypt, with extensive expertise spanning adult and pediatric orthopedics, trauma management, bone deformity correction, joint replacement, and sports-related injuries.

As a Lecturer at Al-Azhar University Faculty of Medicine, Dr. El Zahed bridges rigorous clinical practice with academic research. He has published several peer-reviewed studies in the Al-Azhar International Medical Journal, focusing on advanced minimally invasive orthopedic techniques including MIPO fixation and intramedullary methods.

Dr. El Zahed sees patients at two locations in Cairo — his Nasr City private clinic and Dar Al Fouad Hospital — providing patients across the region with accessible, high-quality orthopedic care.

Specializations

A comprehensive range of orthopedic services — from minimally invasive procedures to complex reconstructive surgery.

Arthroscopy

Minimally invasive diagnostic and surgical arthroscopic procedures for the knee, shoulder, hip, and ankle joints.

Trauma & Fracture Management

Treatment of complex fractures, non-unions, and intramedullary and minimally invasive plate fixation techniques.

Pediatric & Adult Orthopedics

Comprehensive orthopedic care for patients of all ages, from childhood growth disorders to adult joint conditions.

Joint Replacement

Total and partial replacement of hip, knee, and other major joints to restore mobility and eliminate chronic pain.

Sports Injuries

Diagnosis and surgical treatment of ligament tears, cartilage damage, tendon injuries, and other sports-related conditions.

Deformity Correction

Correction of congenital and acquired skeletal deformities using advanced reconstructive and fixation techniques.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common patient questions. For anything else, call the clinic directly.

Most patients return home the same day. Full recovery typically takes 4–6 weeks for daily activities and 3–6 months for sports. Physical therapy is usually recommended.

You will need a thorough clinical evaluation, relevant imaging (X-ray, MRI), and routine lab work. Patients are advised to stop smoking and manage chronic conditions prior to surgery.

Arthroscopy is performed under anesthesia so you will not feel pain during the procedure. Post-operative discomfort is usually mild and managed with prescribed medication.

No referral is required. You can book an appointment directly through this site or call the clinic. All new patients receive a comprehensive consultation.
